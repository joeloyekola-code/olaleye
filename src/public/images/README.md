# Image Guide for Rev. Peter Olaleye Charity Foundation

Place your images in this folder. The website expects the following image files:

## Required Images

### 1. **community.jpg** (Hero Section)
- **Location**: Top of the homepage
- **Dimensions**: At least 800x600px (aspect ratio ~16:9)
- **Description**: A photo showing community members, volunteers, or the foundation in action
- **Usage**: Main hero image showcasing community impact

### 2. **founder.jpg** (About Section)
- **Location**: About section (left side on desktop)
- **Dimensions**: At least 400x500px (portrait orientation)
- **Description**: A professional photo of Reverend Peter Olaleye
- **Usage**: Founder's profile image in the "Our Story" section

### 3. **healthcare.jpg** (Stories Section)
- **Location**: Healthcare story card
- **Dimensions**: At least 800x600px
- **Description**: Photo from a healthcare outreach, medical event, or healthcare-related activity
- **Usage**: Featured image for the "A Mother of Four" story

### 4. **scholarship.jpg** (Stories Section)
- **Location**: Scholarship story card
- **Dimensions**: At least 800x600px
- **Description**: Photo of scholarship recipients, educational event, or student-focused activity
- **Usage**: Featured image for the "A Champion Who Needed a Champion" story

## Important Notes

- All images should be JPG format
- Use high-quality photos (at least 72 dpi resolution)
- Files are case-sensitive on deployment
- Keep file sizes reasonable (under 2MB each) for fast loading
- Ensure images represent the foundation's work and values
- Consider using diverse, authentic community photos

## Adding More Images

To add additional images in the future:
1. Place the image in this folder
2. Update the corresponding component in `src/App.jsx`
3. Use the format: `/images/filename.jpg`

## Image Optimization Tips

- Use tools like TinyPNG or ImageOptim to compress images
- Ensure faces are clearly visible
- Use natural lighting when possible
- Maintain consistent color grading across images
- Consider the mobile view - cropping may occur on smaller screens
